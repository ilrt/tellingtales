Current Website Analysis

Strengths
  • Clean, minimalist design
  • Beautiful 3D map visualization
  • Authentic Caribbean folklore content
  • Audio/video storytelling format
  • Behind-the-scenes interviews (2 stories)
  • Reliable GitHub Pages hosting
Areas for Improvement
  • Limited to St. Lucia only
  • Only 4 story locations
  • Minimal interactive features
  • No community engagement
  • Limited search/filtering

Caribbean Folklore Research

Extensive research into storytelling traditions across the Caribbean reveals rich opportunities for platform expansion.

Priority Islands
  • Jamaica: Anansi stories, Duppy tales
  • Trinidad & Tobago: Papa Bois, La Diablesse
  • Barbados: Heart Man, Bajan folklore
  • Puerto Rico: Taíno legends, El Chupacabra
Story Formats
  • Crick-Crack: Call-and-response storytelling
  • Oral Poetry: Yoruba cultural traditions
  • Musical Tales: Integration with drumming
  • Ceremonial: Religious and cultural stories
Cultural Elements
  • African Heritage: Trickster characters
  • Indigenous: Taíno and Carib traditions
  • European: Colonial period influences
  • Modern: Contemporary urban legends
